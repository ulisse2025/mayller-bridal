4. BRAND VISION / DNA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Pronovias presents itself as a luxury bridal house with deep artisanal roots.
Key brand pillars visible across the site:

  - The Atelier: "Craftsmanship and design intertwine in our Barcelona workshop,
    a unique creative haven where dreams are meticulously transformed into
    exquisite pieces of art." (meta-description of /atelier page)

  - La Maison: dedicated section on brand history and house identity
    (URL: /la-maison)

  - Pronovias for Protection: social responsibility initiative
    (URL: /pronovias-for-protection)

  - "The Art of Craftsmanship — A journey inside the creative heart of Pronovias"
    (homepage scroll section linking to /atelier)

  - "Pronovias for Protection" — sustainability/ethical angle

Brand tone: refined, aspirational, emotionally connected, luxury without excess.
Founded and headquartered in Barcelona, Spain.
Presence: over 3,000 authorized retail locations worldwide.

5. COLORS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The site does not expose raw CSS variables through static extraction,
but the visual identity is clearly identifiable from the brand aesthetic:

  Primary:    White / Ivory — dominant across all backgrounds and dress visuals
  Secondary:  Black — used for navigation text, CTAs, logo, typography
  Accent:     Warm beige / champagne — implied by the product palette
  Highlight:  Gold / champagne tones — visible in Atelier collection imagery

Logo: black wordmark on white, minimal sans-serif
Typography style: clean, uppercase for navigation items, lowercase for body text

Notable: No bright colors or aggressive gradients. The entire palette is
built on neutral elegance — white, black, cream, and soft gold.

8. SHADERS / BACKGROUNDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Visual treatment across the site (inferred from structure and brand):

  - Hero section: full-screen video background, dark overlay/vignette
    gradient applied over video to allow white text legibility
  - Product sections: pure white or very light ivory backgrounds
  - Editorial/Atelier sections: deep black or dark charcoal backgrounds
    used for contrast with imagery (visible in "The Art of Craftsmanship" block)
  - Section transitions: clean white-to-white, no aggressive gradients
  - Announcement bar: minimal dark background with white text
  - Footer: dark/black background with white/light text

The overall approach is: minimal shaders, relying on full-bleed photography
and videography rather than background effects. Overlays are subtle and
used only where text needs contrast.
